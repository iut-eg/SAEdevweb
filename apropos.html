The General Data Protection Regulation

What is the GDPR?

The GDPR, which stands for General Data Protection Regulation, is a regulation put in place by the European Parliament and the Council on 27 April 2016. It is a text relating to the processing of personal data of natural persons, concerning companies and administrations as well as simple users.

What is personal data?

Personal data is information that directly or indirectly identifies a natural person. For example, the name and surname of a natural person is information that directly identifies that person, and his or her activity on the Internet, these identifiers on certain services and others allow him or her to be identified indirectly. The GDPR stipulates that such data may not be used in any way and by anyone, in order to protect individuals with regard to the processing of their data.
