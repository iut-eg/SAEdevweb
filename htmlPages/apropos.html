The General Data Protection Regulation

What is the GDPR?

The GDPR, for General Data Protection Regulation, is a regulation put in place by the European Parliament and the Council on 27 April 2016, it is a text relating to the processing of personal data of individuals, concerning both companies and administrations as well as simple users, stipulating that from this date, the management of personal data by the various organisations will have to be regulated. Several conditions must be fulfilled to use personal data under penalty of serious punishment, consult the Cnil or the government website for more information.

What is personal data?

Personal data is information that directly or indirectly identifies a natural person. For example, a person's first and last name is directly identifiable information, and his or her activity on the internet, identifiers on certain services and other information can be indirectly identified. The GDPR stipulates that this data may not be used in any way and by anyone, in order to protect individuals with regard to the processing of their data.

The framework of this data

Since the advent of the GDPR in 2016, organisations must allow their customers or users concerned by the use of personal data to exercise control over it in order to guarantee maximum protection of this data and a certain degree of portability.

Who is affected by this law?

Organisations, whether public or private, residing in Europe or having European customers must respect and apply this law, under penalty of heavy fines and sanctions. European individuals also have to respect and apply this law or risk prosecution.

How to enforce your rights?

Every individual has the right to control and decide how and by whom their data is used, if it appears that an organisation or individual is using or processing your personal data without your consent, it is your absolute right to sue them or to negotiate the use of your data.

How to protect your personal data on the internet?

Several conditions are required to ensure maximum protection on the internet, you must obviously avoid consulting malicious or insecure sites, then you must be sure to consult the charter of use that is proposed to you when you wish to use your data, in order to keep control over it. Finally, please ensure maximum confidentiality by securing your accounts with secure passwords and by using anti-hacking browsers to navigate the web.
